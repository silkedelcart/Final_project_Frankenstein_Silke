Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein

Mini Digital Edition

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein. Made as a project for Text as Data, taught at the University of Antwerp.

Frankenstein, or the Modern Prometheus by Mary Shelley was first published in 1818. Mary Shelley was 19 years when she started writing this gothic novel on a stormy nicht in the Swiss Alpes. She was there with her husband, Percy Shelley, and their friend Lord Byron. They each decided to write a ghost story. The product of that decision is Frankenstein. The subtiltle or, the Modern Prometheus refers to the scientist from Greek mythology who teaches medicine and science, steals fire from Zeus and gives it to humanity, or creates a human beaing from clay. In the novel, Frankenstein creates life, challenging God, and he is being punished for it, because the creature kills some of his close relatives and friends, including his bride on his wedding night (source).

This mini digital edition contains a selection of pages from the Frankenstein Volume I Draft in Chapter Sequence manuscript (Bodleian Shelley MS. 2). The manuscript is held at the Bodleian Library in Oxford. The digital edition was created using the BDMP encoding scheme and Mirador viewer. The transcription and encoding were done based on the Shelley Godwin Archive's transcription. In this part of the story, Frankenstein has just created his creature. He is frightened by it and runs. Later, he meets Clerval, his childhood friend, who comes to visit him. However, the stress of the past days gets to Frankenstein, and he passes out.
